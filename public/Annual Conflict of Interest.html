ANNUAL CONFLICT OF INTEREST

Purpose: To ensure transparency and integrity, all permanent employees must annually declare any potential conflicts of interest. This includes the use of company position and assets for personal gain and relationships with relatives in the organization or vendors.

Scope: This policy applies to all permanent employees

Annual Declaration

  • Employees must complete the Conflict-of-Interest Declaration Form by the end of March each year.  (Refer Annexure 1.7)
  • The form should be submitted to the HR department.

Key Declarations

  • No Self-Interest Use: Employees must declare they are not using their position or company assets for personal gain.
  • Relatives in the Organization: Employees must disclose if they have any relatives working in the company, providing details such as name, relationship, designation, and department.
  • Vendor Relationships: Employees must disclose if any relatives are employed by a vendor or service provider, detailing the vendor’s name, relationship, nature of service, and potential advantage to the company.

Approval for Potential Conflicts

If there is a potential conflict of interest, employees must declare it and seek approval from the Company Board of Directors / Managing Partners.

Non-Compliance

Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination
