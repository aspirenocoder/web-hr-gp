ROLES AND RESPONSBILITIES

a. Appraisee:

  • The appraisee is required to fill the Performance Appraisal Form (PAF) along with the supporting documents/notes to substantiate the self-ratings as perceived by him/her.
  • Fill in the details of accomplishment against the set targets, reasons for deviation from set targets and complete the final self – ratings. The PAF is then submitted to the Appraiser.

  • Appraisee must not consider the self-appraisal score as the final score.
  • Appraisee must have an open mind to discuss and take the feedback given by the appraiser and the reviewer. Use this opportunity to ask for feedback on your performance, and for further support on your development.

  • Appraisee will put forth all his expectations and limitations from time to time, without waiting for the year end assessment and seek necessary support from the superiors, for overcoming the limitations and improve his performance measures
  • After you have agreed your rating with your Appraiser, sign-off on the performance rating sheet.

b. Appraiser:

  • Ensure your direct reports understand the performance management process
  • The appraiser is required to evaluate the performance of the appraisee during the appraisal period after concluding the Performance Review Discussion with the appraisee.
  • The appraiser is then required to mention his performance evaluation of the appraisee in the PAF (by duly completing it as per the guidelines mentioned therein).
  • After duly completing the form, the appraiser is required to submit the completed PAF to the Reviewer for review, within such time that would permit the Reviewer to do the appraisal review and return duly completed appraisal forms to the HR Department.
  • In case the appraisee has, during the appraisal period, worked under more than one supervisor, in such case the appraiser (i.e. the current supervisor) is required to discuss/take the inputs with respect to the appraisee's performance from the previous supervisor, and mention the same in the appraisal form.
  • Communicate to the appraisee if any changes are being made to the rating by the Reviewer

c. Reviewer:

  • Ensure that all Appraisers adequately prepare themselves before they carry out the appraisal process with individuals in an unbiased and objective manner.
  • The Reviewer is required to mention his remarks with respect to the appraisal process i.e. about the appraisal conducted by the appraiser with respect to the appraisee.
  • This would include his remarks on the fairness of the appraisal process, remarks on whether the appraiser upheld/did not uphold the letter and the spirit with which the appraisal process was supposed to be carried out, any amendments he would wish to make to the performance evaluation of the appraisee by the appraiser and/or any additional remarks he has to offer in relation to the appraisal process and which he may like to bring to the notice of the HR Department
  • In case the Reviewer makes any amendments in the form, he should communicate the same to the concerned appraiser and appraisee.
  • After completing the Appraisal Review, the Reviewer is expected to submit the completed Performance Appraisal form to the HR Department directly or through the concerned HOD.

8.6 PERFORMANCE FEEDBACK:

  • Although, performance feedback is part of the performance appraisal process, it must be an ongoing event. Performance feedback must be constantly provided by the Appraiser on completion of a project or on periodic intervals (atleast half-yearly).
  • Appraisee’s also must ask for feedback from their appraiser regularly.
  • Reviewer must objectively assess any cases of conflict between Appraiser and Appraisee and provide inputs to resolve such conflicts without any prejudice to both parties
  • As part of this discussion, inputs regarding trainings and development initiatives can be provided by the appraiser. This will help in motivation and further improve an employee’s performance and help him/her prepare for career progression. This will be a structured meeting in an environment of trust and with a view to developing the employee.
