CHILDREN HIGHER EDUCATION FUND POLICY

1. Policy Overview

The Children Higher Education Fund Policy ("Policy") is designed to support the continuing education of the children of our employees. This Policy aims to provide financial assistance for higher education, reinforcing our commitment to the families of our employees and investing in the future of our community. This assistance is funded through our Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) allocations and is a testament to our commitment to the well-being of our employees and their families.

2. Eligibility Criteria

  1. Employee Eligibility: This benefit is available to all permanent, full-time employees who have completed at least five consecutive years of service with the company by the application deadline date.
  2. Beneficiary Eligibility: The beneficiary, i.e., the child of the eligible employee, must be enrolled in or accepted for full-time study at an accredited higher education institution.

3. Application Process

  1. Employees must apply by the specified deadline each year, including documentation of their child's acceptance or enrollment in a higher education program.
  2. The application must include an essay by the employee detailing how this fund would benefit their child's educational goals and a brief overview of their financial need.
  3. The management reserves the right to request additional documentation or information as deemed necessary.

4. Selection Criteria

The management committee, appointed by the company's executive team, will review applications based on the following criteria:

  1. Demonstrated financial need.
  2. Academic achievement of the child.
  3. Potential impact of the fund on the child's educational goals.

5. Award Decision

The management committee has full discretion to select the applications for funding. This decision may involve funding one, multiple, or no applications in any given year, based on the availability of CSR funds and the merit of applications received.

  1. The committee may decide to award the fund fully or partially, depending on the CSR fund's status and the number of deserving applications.
  2. No amount of fund or number of awards is promised in advance.

6. Award Distribution

  1. The fund, once awarded, will be directly disbursed to the educational institution under the student's account to be used towards tuition, books, and other academic-related fees.
  2. Recipients and the amount awarded will be announced annually at a company event dedicated to this purpose.

7. Terms and Conditions

  1. The fund awarded is a one-time benefit per child, but employees may reapply in subsequent years for the same or different children, subject to all eligibility criteria being met.
  2. The company reserves the right to modify, suspend, or terminate this Policy at any time, with or without notice, as it deems necessary.
  3. All decisions by the management committee regarding the selection of awardees and the amount of funding are final and not subject to appeal.

8. Repayment Clause / Condition of Repayment:

  1. If an employee who has received funding under this policy leaves the company within one year from the date of fund disbursement, they are required to repay the entire amount funded.
  2. The Children Higher Education Fund is awarded with the expectation that the beneficiary (the child of the eligible employee) successfully completes their course of study. In the event that the beneficiary withdraws from the program, fails to maintain satisfactory academic progress as defined by the educational institution, or is otherwise unable to complete their studies, a prorated portion of the funds may be subject to repayment to the company. The specifics of this repayment will be determined based on the portion of the educational program completed at the time of withdrawal or failure to progress.
  3. Repayment Terms: Should repayment be required, the employee will be notified in writing of the amount to be repaid and the reasons for this decision. The company will provide a reasonable repayment schedule, not to exceed 3 months from the date of notification, taking into consideration the employee’s circumstances.

9. Review and Appeal:

  1. Applicants will receive written communication regarding the outcome of their application.
  2. Employees may request a review of the decision by submitting a written appeal to the HR department within 30 days of receiving the decision.

10.  Confidentiality & Amendments:

  1. All personal and financial information submitted for the purpose of this application will be treated with the utmost confidentiality and used solely for the purpose of determining eligibility and merit for the Children Higher Education Fund.
  2. This policy is subject to review and amendment by the management to ensure its effectiveness and alignment with the company's goals and CSR initiatives.
