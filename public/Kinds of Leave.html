KINDS OF LEAVE

  1. SICK LEAVE (SL):
  1. The company grants 10 number of sick leave days per calendar year to employees.
  2. Employees who are not covered in the ESI act are entitled for sick leave for the purpose of addressing personal illness or medical treatments.
  3. Employees will be eligible for SL only after confirmation. For such employees SL will be provided based on pro-rata basis after confirmation.
  4. Employees covered under ESI act will claim benefit as per ESI act. They need to submit the medical certificate to ESI to claim the salary for period of absence due to personal sickness.
  5. Employees shall be eligible for a maximum of 3 days sick leave per month and if unapplied, these can be accrued to the following month.
  6. On events where the leave exceeds 3 days, an employee must produce the medical certificate before resuming work.
  7. Un-utilized sick leaves will not be carried forward or encashed. It will get lapsed at the end of every calendar year.
  8. Sick leaves cannot be clubbed with casual leave but can be availed along with earned leaves with due authorization of granting authority.

  1. CASUAL LEAVE:
  1. Casual leave is for unexpected events, such as medical appointments, family emergencies or any other personal work.
  2. The Company grants 12 days of casual leave in a calendar year for each employee.
  3. Casual leave cannot be accumulated, un-availed CL will automatically lapse at the end of calendar year.
  4. Employees should promptly notify their supervisor while availing CL.
  5. Casual leave can be taken for minimum half-day and Max 3 days. More than 3 days’ casual leave will not be allowed at a time.
  6. Casual leave will be credited to the employees at the beginning of the calendar year (January) and can be availed on pro-rata basis.
  7. Casual leave cannot be combined with sick leave, earned leave, maternity leave, and paternity leaves.
  8. Sundays/weekly-offs/comp-offs can be prefixed/ suffixed to casual leave/earned leave/sick leave.
  9. Casual Leave can be taken while on official trip, but no travel allowance will be applicable for that period.
  10. If a person joins in the middle of a year, casual leave will be granted to him on pro-rata basis.

  1. EARNED LEAVE:
  1. Employees who have completed a minimum of 240 days service in a calendar year are entitled to Earned Leave.
  2. These minimum 240 days do not include Maternity/Paternity leaves, Accidents in the workplace and Lockout.
  3. Calculation of Earned Leave will be one day for every 20 working days (fractions will be ignored).
  4. A maximum of 14 days per year will be allowed as EL, provided that the employee is physically present for the required number of days as cited by the management.
  5. Earned leave can be availed only for 3 occasions per year.
  6. Only 30 days will be allowed to carry forward to the next year. Rest will be encashed.
  7. Employees must get permission from their department head/supervisor 7 days prior to the date of leave.
  8. Days of unauthorised absence and days on which the employee is on loss of pay will not be considered for the purpose of calculating leave eligibility.

  1. COMPENSATORY OFF:
  1. When employees work beyond their regular working hours during any normal working day, on weekends or during public holidays, they might be eligible for "compensatory off" hours as per Annexure 5.3 given below.
  2. Compensatory off can be availed only when the supervisor at his/her discretion requests the employee to work on holiday/weekend. The employee cannot decide to work on a holiday and then seek compensatory off.
  3. Supervisor/HOD should intimate an employee in advance, if he/she needs to work on public holiday or weekly off, with prior approval from Business Head/ Factory head/ Managing Partner.
  4. HR shall verify employee’s presence on said days with biometric attendance recorded.
  5. Supervisors are advised to use their discretion and minimise instances of Compensatory off requirements. This is to enable employees to have time to rest every week from work.
  6. An employee is allowed to earn a maximum of 3 compensatory offs in a month. HOD must ensure that employee takes the comp off earned within a week or atleast in the same month.
  7. Compensatory off that involve Travel/Tour:

  1. If any employee travels for official purposes during his/her week off/public holiday it should involve meeting clients, spending time at the site.
  2. Time spent travelling will not be considered as part of working on a holiday/week off.

  1. OVERTIME

  1. Eligibility for overtime requires the completion of regular working hours in a day.
  2. Employees must work a minimum of 2 hours beyond their regular hours to eligible for overtime pay.
  3. All factory employees upto executive cadre (Grade 18 to 10) will be eligible for Overtime pay.
  4. Company drivers will not be eligible to claim overtime pay. They will be given incentives/allowances during season as per Seasonal Allowance Policy.
  5. Overtime will be paid only upon clear validation of punching history and of the productivity levels during said hours by the Head of the department/Supervisor/HR Department
  6. Overtime payment will be included in the employee's monthly salary.
  7. Prior approval from the reporting manager or department head is necessary for overtime work.
  8. Overtime pay will adhere to the rates specified by labour regulations.
  9. Accurate records of employee overtime hours will be maintained.
  10. Employees are responsible for accurately reporting their overtime hours.
  11. Overtime will not be calculated if an employee misses to punch in their attendance.
  12. Periodic review and updates of the policy to align with labour regulations and organisational needs.

  1. MATERNITY LEAVE:
  1. Applicable for all female employees during:
  1. Pregnancy: 180 days. - Applicable only to employees with less than two surviving children.
  2. Miscarriage/abortion (induced or otherwise): Total of 45 days in the entire service.
  1. The leave application in ESS should be supported by a certificate from a Registered Medical Practitioner.
  2. Employee will get their regular salary during this period.
  3. It is counted as service for increments.
  4. It is counted as service for pension. It is not admissible for ‘threatened abortion’.
  5. The employee must submit the hospital discharge certificate and a copy of birth certificate of the child after availing the maternity leave. Details of child should be given for name incorporation in medical insurance policy as soon as possible.

  1. PATERNITY LEAVE (CHILDBIRTH SUPPORT LEAVE FOR MALE PARENT):
  1. Employee can take a maximum of 50% against the available sick leave balance for the paternity leave.  
  2. Prior approval of the concerned head is required along with doctor's certificates which should be forwarded to the HR department.

  1. MENSTRUAL LEAVE:
  1. Gourmet Popcornica encourages a supportive environment where all employees, regardless of gender, understand and respect the purpose of menstrual leave.
  2. This is applicable only for female employees who work in nurseries farm, R & D and factory at Andhra Pradesh location and are eligible for three days menstrual leave in a month.
  3. This leave is given apart from sick leave or other annual leave balances.
  4. Employees should inform their immediate supervisor or the HR department of their intention to take menstrual leave as early as possible to allow for adequate work adjustments.
  5. Employees facing severe symptoms that require more than the allocated leave may use their sick leave.
